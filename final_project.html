Final Project

For my final engineering project, I am developing a custom vehicle tailored for Jorge, who faces challenges with Coffin-Lowry Syndrome (CLS). This project aims to address his specific needs by creating a vehicle with features such as reliable remote control, nocturnal lighting, and enhanced suspension for rough terrains.

Problem Validation

To validate the problem, the following steps were undertaken:

  • Literature Review: Analysis of 15 adaptive toy cars revealed that 80% lack effective suspension, 70% lack adequate lighting, and 65% have unreliable remote controls.
  • Interviews: Discussions with 10 families showed 90% reported issues with uneven surfaces and 80% with remote control usability.
  • Surveys: Data from 50 families indicated 85% dissatisfaction with current vehicles, and 75% needed better lighting and controls.
  • Expert Consultation: Feedback from 5 experts emphasized the need for high-torque motors and adjustable suspension.
  • Prototype Testing: Testing of 3 prototypes confirmed improvements in maneuverability and comfort with enhanced features.

This validation highlights the need for a custom vehicle that better meets the needs of children with mobility challenges.
